Nagpur to Mumbai: the ₹620 option MakeMyTrip won't show you first

We compared every realistic way to travel this route. The cheapest flight costs ₹4,200. The Vidarbha Express AC 3-Tier costs ₹620 and gets you to Mumbai by morning. Here's the full breakdown.

This route came up in our first week of taking WhatsApp queries. A college student in Nagpur wanted to visit family in Mumbai over a long weekend. He'd already checked MakeMyTrip — the first results were flights starting at ₹3,800. He messaged us asking if there was a cheaper way.

There was. Six of them, actually.

All 6 options, compared honestly

Why we recommend the Vidarbha Express

The AC 3 Tier on this train is genuinely comfortable — individual berths, charging points, blanket included. You board in the evening, sleep, and wake up in Mumbai. No hotel night needed. That alone saves most people ₹800–₹1,500 compared to arriving the day before a flight.

The Sewagram Sleeper at ₹280 is real and valid if your budget is very tight. It's less comfortable but it works — and ₹280 for a journey of this distance is remarkable. We're not going to hide it just because the AC option earns us more (it earns us nothing — neither does).

When the flight actually makes sense

If you book a flight 3–4 weeks in advance, Spicejet and Akasa regularly show ₹2,200–₹2,500. At that price, with the time saved, it becomes a reasonable call — especially if you're travelling for work and need to arrive fresh. Last-minute flights at ₹4,500+ almost never make sense on this route.

Our honest take

For most students and young professionals on this route: Vidarbha Express AC 3-Tier is the answer. Book on IRCTC directly (train number 12105). If tatkal quota is open, the surcharge is around ₹200 extra — still far cheaper than flying.

The last-mile gap we noticed

Several people who messaged us about this route were actually from smaller towns near Nagpur — Wardha, Amravati, Yavatmal — where getting to Nagpur railway station itself is a hassle. This is the gap no app solves. We're actively working on a shared pickup solution for this corridor.
